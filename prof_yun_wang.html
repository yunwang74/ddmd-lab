Professor Yun Wang

Principal Investigator | School of Environment and Science, Griffith University

Professor Yun Wang leads the Data-Driven Materials Design Group at Griffith University. His research combines computational modelling, data science, and experimental studies to understand and engineer functional materials for clean energy and environmental applications. A significant focus of his recent work is on wastewater remediation, including electrocatalytic processes for extracting critical metals from mining wastewater and the degradation of organic pollutants.

By integrating first-principles and Grand Canonical DFT (GC-DFT), statistical mechanics, and advanced materials characterisation, his team investigates how the structure and active sites of materials govern their properties. Much of this deep theoretical focus is applied to understanding complex electrified solid-liquid interfacial properties

Furthermore, Professor Wang is actively expanding into "AI for Science", utilising advanced machine learning tools to accelerate the predictive design and discovery of next-generation materials for catalysis and renewable energy technologies.

He is a Fellow of both the Royal Society of Chemistry (FRSC) and the Royal Australian Chemical Institute (FRACI). His research has yielded over 200 peer-reviewed papers with more than 20,000 citations, including publications in leading journals such as Nature and Nature Energy.
