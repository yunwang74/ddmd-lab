Opportunities

Join a supportive, interdisciplinary research group

We welcome students and researchers who want to work on computational materials, data-driven discovery, and real-world applications in energy and the environment.

The Data-Driven Materials Design Group brings together statistical mechanics, quantum chemistry, molecular simulation, and data science to study surfaces, interfaces, and functional materials under realistic conditions.

If you are curious, motivated, and enjoy collaborative problem solving, we would be glad to hear from you. You do not need to wait for a perfectly matched vacancy to make contact.

Good fit for: people with interests in DFT, molecular simulation, electrochemistry, materials chemistry, physics, data analysis, machine learning, or related fields.

What we welcome

  • PhD candidates
    We are keen to hear from students with backgrounds in chemistry, physics, materials science, chemical engineering, data science, or related areas.
  • Honours and Masters students
    We can support projects that combine theory, computation, and practical materials problems.
  • Postdoctoral researchers and visitors
    We welcome researchers interested in interfaces, electrochemistry, multiscale modelling, and data-driven materials design.
  • Collaborators
    If you have an idea for a joint project, method development effort, or application-focused study, please get in touch.

Project themes

Examples of the kinds of projects that often fit the lab's expertise.

  • Grand-canonical DFT
  • Interfacial electrochemistry
  • Surface reactions
  • Molecular simulation
  • Machine learning for materials
  • Energy conversion
  • Battery materials
  • Environmental sensing
  • Remediation chemistry

What to send

  • Short introduction
    Tell us what interests you, what type of role you are seeking, and why the group appeals to you.
  • CV or resume
    Include education, research experience, publications, coding or modelling skills, and any relevant industry experience.
  • Academic record
    If you are enquiring about a student position, an unofficial transcript is helpful.
  • Optional links
    ORCID, GitHub, thesis links, or sample code can help us understand your background more quickly.

How we support new members

  • Clear project matching
    We work with you to shape a project that fits your skills, goals, and available support.
  • Regular mentoring
    We aim to provide a welcoming environment with regular feedback and realistic milestones.
  • Scholarship guidance
    When relevant, we can discuss how your project aligns with scholarship or funding applications.
  • Interdisciplinary collaboration
    We encourage work across theory, simulation, data, and experiment whenever it strengthens the project.
